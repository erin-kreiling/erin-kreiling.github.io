The University of Maryland maintains an extensive database, called the Global Terrorism Database, that details any and all terrorist events from 1970-2017. Within the database, there is information on more than 180,000 terrorist attacks with a variety of different factors, such as Date, Region, Country, Perpetrator Group, Weapon Type, Attack Type, and Target Type. The data for each of the attacks collected from more than 4 million news articles and 25,000 other news sources. Citation information for the database can be found at the bottom of the page.

Data Preparation and Preliminary Analysis The initial data mining for this project was completed within RStudio. After downloading .csv files from the University of Maryland Global Terrorism Database, each was loaded into RStudio then concatenated to form one large .csv file with the appropriate variables and years included. The larger dataset was then analyzed to ensure proper variable types and identify any missing values. The Codebook provided by the database was utilized extensively during the data mining and cleaning portion of the project. The majority of the cleaning for this project took place within Excel, working with pivot tables to filter the data into the necessary subsets, as well as filling in a minor number of missing values. Some of the data was loaded into Google Sheets, in order to make use of the Google Charts API. However, because the data coming from the University database was so clean, minimal data munging was necessary and the focus was primarily on developing the visualizations nad this website.

Citation: National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START). (2018). Global Terrorism Database [Data file]. Retrieved from https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd
